### **1. Core Clinical Philosophy**
The "VetCardioHub approach" prioritises **early detection, proactive monitoring, and a collaborative partnership** between pet owners and veterinary professionals. Clinical staging (ACVIM stages B1, B2, C, D) is considered more critical for management decisions than the specific type of cardiomyopathy.

*   **The 5 Pillars of Monitoring:** Owners are encouraged to track: (1) Sleeping Respiratory Rate (SRR), (2) Breathing Effort, (3) Appetite, (4) Body Weight, and (5) Activity Levels.
*   **The Importance of SRR:** The **Sleeping Respiratory Rate (SRR)** is the most powerful early warning sign of **Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)**. It is more reliable than coughing, as the deep parts of the lungs where fluid builds up have few cough receptors.
*   **Balanced View on Wearable Tech:** Wearable devices are recommended for owners who have difficulty manual counting or who want to spot long-term health and activity trends. While useful for monitoring, they do not replace professional echocardiography, which remains the "gold standard" for diagnosis.

### **4. Key Cardiology Reference Data**
This section contains the specific metrics and validated brand information used by VetCardioHub.

**A. Respiratory Thresholds (SRR)**
| Status | Breaths Per Minute (bpm) | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Normal** | 15–30 bpm (Typically 15–25) | Record baseline trends. |
| **Concern** | Consistently >30 bpm | Contact veterinarian for advice. |
| **Urgent** | 40 bpm or higher | Immediate emergency veterinary contact. |

**B. Radiographic Metrics (VHS & VLAS)**
*   **Vertebral Heart Size (VHS) - Dogs:** Normal range is **8.4 to 10.5**. A value **>10.5** indicates probable enlargement.
*   **Vertebral Left Atrial Size (VLAS) - Dogs:** Normal range is **<2.3**. Values **>2.5** suggest enlargement; **>3.0** indicates high likelihood of left atrial/ventricular enlargement.
*   **Vertebral Heart Size (VHS) - Cats:** Normal Lateral is **6.9 to 8.1**. Normal Ventrodorsal (VD) is **7.3 to 9.1**.

**C. Validated Technology & Brand Names**
*   **Monitoring Apps:** **Cardalis** (free app for tracking SRR).
*   **Wearable Trackers:** **Maven Pet** (smart health tracker for heart/respiratory rates).
*   **Diagnostics:** **AliveCor** (smartphone ECG), **eKuore One** (digital stethoscope attachment).

**D. Core Medications**
*   **Inodilator:** **Pimobendan** (Vetmedin)—standard for stage B2, C, and D in dogs.
*   **Diuretics:** **Furosemide/Frusemide** (first line) and **Torasemide/Torsemide** (potent, used for advanced stages).
*   **Antithrombotics:** **Clopidogrel**, **Rivaroxaban**, and **Aspirin** (especially for cats with large left atria).
