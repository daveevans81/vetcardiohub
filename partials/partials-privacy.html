Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 July 2026

VetCardioHub (“we”, “us”, or “our”) respects your privacy. This policy explains what personal data we collect, how we use it, and the rights you have under data protection laws including the UK GDPR and EU GDPR. It covers both our website / mailing list and the VetCardioHub tracker application.

1. The Health Tracker Application

Data you enter into the VetCardioHub Health tracker — patient profiles, resting respiratory rates, medications, activity, cough, syncope, staging and other clinical logs — is stored solely in your browser’s local storage on your own device. It is never transmitted to, collected by, or accessible to us, and it is not held on any server we operate.

  • Because this data never leaves your device, we cannot see it, recover it, or restore it for you.
  • Clearing your browser data, uninstalling the app, or losing the device will permanently and irrecoverably delete your records. You are responsible for keeping backups using the app’s export / Master Backup tools.
  • Where you record another person’s details (for example an owner’s name), you act as the controller of that information and are responsible for handling it lawfully.

If we ever introduce a feature that transmits or stores tracker data off your device, we will update this policy and tell you before you use it.

2. The VetCardioHub EchoCalculator Application.

  • Data you enter into the VetCardioHub echocalculator (patient profiles, names, report information and clinical notes) is stored solely in your browser's local storage on your own device.
  • It is never transmitted to, collected by, or accessible to us.
  • Because of this, you control that data entirely, you are responsible for keeping records using the export tools provided, and clearing your browser data or a simple page refresh will permanently delete it.
  • Where you record another person's details (e.g. an owner's name), you act as the controller of that information.

3. Information We Collect (Website & Mailing List)

Separately from the apps on the site, when you interact with our website we may collect:

  • Email address – when you sign up to receive our Dog Murmur Action Plan, tutorial emails, or other updates.
  • Optional details – if you provide information such as your pet’s type or ACVIM stage.
  • Technical information – Brevo (our email provider) records your IP address, browser type, and the date/time of signup for security and consent records.

We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 16.

4. How We Use Your Information

  • Send you the Dog Murmur Action Plan you requested.
  • Deliver a short series of follow-up emails to help you use the guide.
  • Send occasional updates or new resources only if you have given consent (by ticking the optional checkbox on the signup form).
  • Maintain accurate subscriber records and comply with legal obligations.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

Under data protection law, we rely on:

  • Consent – when you opt in to receive updates or newsletters.
  • Legitimate interest / performance of your request – to send the resource and follow-up tutorial emails you specifically asked for.

6. Sharing Your Information

We use trusted third-party services to operate our website and email list:

  • Brevo (Sendinblue) – email marketing and automation platform.
  • Cloudflare – website hosting and security provider.

These providers act as our data processors and only process your information on our instructions. We do not sell or trade your personal data. (Note: these services relate to the website and mailing list only — your tracker data, as described in section 1, is never shared because it never leaves your device.)

Third-Party Content Delivery

All software libraries, fonts and icons used by our applications are served directly from our own hosting. Beyond our hosting and security provider (Cloudflare, named above), loading and using the applications sends no requests to, and shares no data with, any third-party content delivery network.

7. Data Retention

We keep your email address for as long as you remain subscribed or until you ask us to delete it. Unsubscribing from emails will stop all future messages, but Brevo may retain minimal records to demonstrate compliance with legal obligations. Tracker data is retained only on your own device, under your control, for as long as you choose to keep it.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction or deletion of your data.
  • Withdraw consent at any time (unsubscribe link in every email).
  • Request a copy of your data in a portable format.
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority — in the UK, the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk, or your local data-protection authority if you are elsewhere.

To exercise these rights, please contact us at privacy@vetcardiohub.com.

9. International Data Transfers

Brevo’s servers may be located outside the UK/EU. Where data is transferred internationally, appropriate safeguards (such as adequacy decisions or Standard Contractual Clauses) are applied to protect it.

10. Cookies & Local Storage

The health tracker application uses your browser’s local storage to save your records on your device. This is strictly necessary for the app to function and does not track you or transmit anything to us, so it does not require consent. Our website may also use essential and analytics cookies provided by Cloudflare and our email platform; these are used only to keep the site secure and functioning.

11. Security

For our website and mailing systems, we use encryption and secure, reputable providers to protect data. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. Tracker data is not transmitted at all — its security depends on the security of your own device.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with a revised “last updated” date.
