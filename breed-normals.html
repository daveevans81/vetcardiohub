Why Use Breed-Specific Reference Intervals?

Standard allometric scaling (Cornell method) is reliable for many dogs, but "deviant" breeds—such as Greyhounds, Boxers, and Cavalier King Charles Spaniels—often have physiological heart sizes that fall outside standard multi-breed ranges.

Using breed-specific data helps avoid mis-staging patients with preclinical heart disease (Stage B1 vs B2).
